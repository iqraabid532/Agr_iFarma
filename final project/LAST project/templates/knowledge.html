  • Organic Farming: Methods and benefits.

    • Reduces Chemical Pollution: Avoids synthetic pesticides and fertilizers, which prevents soil and water contamination. Enhances Soil Health: Uses compost, green manure, and crop rotation, improving soil fertility and structure. Biodiversity Conservation: Supports diverse plants, insects, and animals, creating balanced ecosystems. Lower Carbon Footprint: Often relies on local inputs and renewable resources, reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
